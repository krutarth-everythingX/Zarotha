# Design Principles

## Core Direction

Zarokha’s public experience should feel like a premium handcrafted-art catalogue: warm, calm, editorial, image-led, and materially grounded. The site should feel designed around objects, texture, scale, and trust rather than around retail urgency or SaaS efficiency.

## Principles

### 1. Product First

- Let products, materials, and craftsmanship imagery carry the strongest visual weight.
- Keep interface chrome quiet so decorative objects remain the focal point.

### 2. Editorial Calm

- Use generous whitespace, long visual pauses, and restrained hierarchy.
- Prefer fewer, larger decisions over dense blocks of competing UI.

### 3. Warm Structure

- Build the system around warm neutrals, subtle depth, and wood-adjacent accents.
- Final brand colors remain a client dependency, so the system should use semantic color roles rather than hard-wired brand claims.

### 4. Tactile Sophistication

- Surfaces should feel soft, crafted, and premium rather than glossy or highly synthetic.
- Use restrained borders, shallow shadows, and material-sensitive spacing.

### 5. Inquiry Over Transaction

- Every conversion path should guide visitors toward inquiry, WhatsApp, phone, or contact.
- Never imply a cart, checkout, or online purchase flow.

### 6. Readable Luxury

- Typography should feel elegant and editorial without becoming fragile.
- High-contrast serif display moments should pair with practical, accessible text styles.

### 7. Motion With Restraint

- Motion should support atmosphere, orientation, and product appreciation.
- Avoid decorative motion that distracts from browsing or slows interaction.

### 8. Accessibility As Baseline

- Menus, forms, galleries, dialogs, and lightboxes must remain keyboard-usable and screen-reader-friendly.
- Reduced-motion alternatives are part of the system, not an afterthought.

### 9. Responsive By Composition

- Layouts should recompose, not merely shrink.
- Mobile should preserve the same premium tone while simplifying hierarchy and touch behavior.

### 10. Admin Consistency Without Redesign

- The admin CMS should reuse the supplied UI kit directly.
- Any corrections should improve accessibility or clarity without redesigning the kit’s visual language.

## Non-Goals

- No generic ecommerce look and feel.
- No overly bright luxury clichés, hard gradients, or ornamental clutter.
- No public placeholders that pretend missing client content exists.
