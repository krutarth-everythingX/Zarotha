# Image Guidelines

## Image Principles

- Imagery must support a premium handcrafted catalogue experience.
- Images should help visitors understand finish, material, scale, and decorative character.
- Low-quality or inconsistent imagery is a business risk and should not be masked with design tricks.

## Required Image Types

- Homepage hero images
- Collection cover images
- Product card images
- Product featured images
- Product gallery images
- About or craftsmanship supporting images if approved and supplied
- Social preview images for key pages where needed

## Orientation Guidance

- Hero images: wide landscape compositions preferred.
- Collection covers: landscape or portrait may be used, but the system should standardize one primary card ratio later.
- Product cards: consistent ratio across the listing experience.
- Product gallery: support multiple views, but maintain predictable presentation standards.

## Crop Guidance

- Avoid crops that remove the defining silhouette or key handcrafted detail of a product.
- Keep enough negative space for responsive cropping where hero or card layouts may vary.
- Use consistent framing across products in the same family where possible.

## Subject Guidance

- Product images should prioritize the product itself rather than heavy lifestyle staging.
- Include close-up detail shots when they help communicate craftsmanship, carving, finish, or material texture.
- Avoid cluttered backgrounds that reduce legibility.

## Quality Guidance

- Supply images at sufficient resolution for large-detail viewing on product pages.
- Maintain consistent color balance and exposure across the catalogue.
- Avoid visible compression artifacts, blur, or distracting watermarks.

## Alt-Text Rules

- Describe what the visitor needs to understand from the image.
- Keep alt text concise but specific.
- Example pattern: product name plus visible view or detail.
- Avoid keyword stuffing and avoid repeating surrounding caption text unnecessarily.

## File Preparation Guidance

- Preserve originals for future processing where possible.
- Use clearly organized source filenames before upload when available.
- Plan for derived optimized formats in later implementation stages.

## Fallback Rules

- If no approved image exists for an optional page section, hide the section.
- If required product imagery is missing, the product should not be considered launch-ready.
- Do not use generic public placeholder images.
